Plan for the Future.

Longboarding isn't exactly a legal form of transportation. It's a very grey area.
This is bad. Because if you get into an accident, you will likely be at fault. No matter what actually happened. If you're driving on the road, and get hit by a car: you were 'playing on the road', which despite Canada's tolerance for street hockey, is illegal. If you're in a bike lane, you're breaking the law. If you're anywhere on the street other than a crosswalk, you're breaking the law. If you're on a pathway, you need to yield to everyone and everything. We don't have the specific details for Halifax. But they'll be directly linked to this site soon.

But if you're interested, Here is a blog post about the legality of skateboarding in Toronto.
